# Fundamental Law Candidates

| rank | candidate | law-shaped claim |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | State Reachability Theory | Candidate law: agent success is bounded by reachable grounded states under current evidence, tools, and commitments. |
| 2 | Runtime Integrity Theory | Candidate law: success requires preserving evidence-plan-action-final consistency through runtime. |
| 3 | Information Flow Theory | Candidate law: actionable evidence must remain connected to downstream actions. |
| 4 | Decisive Evidence Theory | Candidate law: outcome change concentrates around a small set of evidence events. |
| 5 | Branch Collapse Theory | Candidate law: once branch entropy collapses, recovery probability falls sharply unless intervention occurs. |
