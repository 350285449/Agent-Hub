# Intervention Assessment

Scope: cloud models only. No primitive searches, no interaction searches, no new theories. This assessment consolidates the intervention program.

## Findings

The first intervention opportunity is contradictory grounding in the 25%-50% execution window. This is the earliest measured high-coverage warning sign and corresponds to the first practical Grounding Integrity degradation.

The best overall intervention is grounding confirmation because it covers the dominant union of failures: interpretation errors and action-linkage errors. The best early intervention is contradiction detection and resolution.

The central recoverable estimate is 106.6 of 385 failures, or 27.7%. The theoretical warning ceiling is 226 of 385 failures, or 58.7%. The optimistic high repair estimate is 193.6 failures, or 50.3%.

## Question Answers

1. When is the first intervention opportunity?

At contradictory grounding in the 25%-50% execution window.

2. Which intervention works best?

Grounding confirmation works best overall. Contradiction detection and resolution works best as the earliest targeted intervention.

3. How many failures are recoverable?

The realistic central estimate is 106.6 of 385 failures, or 27.7%.

4. What is the theoretical recovery ceiling?

226 of 385 failures, or 58.7%, bounded by observed failures with measurable warnings after grounding begins.

5. Can Grounding Integrity be actively maintained?

Yes. It can be maintained by repeatedly checking evidence interpretation, contradiction status, evidence verification, evidence-action consistency, and grounded-action ratio during execution.

6. Does intervention outperform prediction?

Yes as an operational control strategy. Prediction remains useful for risk awareness, but live intervention can change the trajectory after grounding begins to deteriorate.

## Final Determination

Failures can often be prevented once Grounding Integrity begins to deteriorate, but prevention is bounded. The realistic central recoverable share is about 27.7% of all failures. The practical program should prioritize early contradiction resolution and a pre-final grounding confirmation gate.
