## Operational Definitions

| category | definition |
| --- | --- |
| evidence not found | no discovered/retrieved evidence signal |
| evidence found but ignored | recognized evidence fails before acceptance or action linkage |
| evidence misinterpreted | retrieved/surfaced evidence does not become understood evidence |
| evidence partially understood | retrieved/surfaced evidence receives weak partial understanding but does not cross the strong-understanding threshold |
| evidence disconnected from action | accepted evidence does not become an action path |
| evidence overridden by prior belief | high prior confidence with recognized evidence but low grounded-action ratio |
| evidence lost during execution | accepted evidence is followed by stuck/switching execution without grounded execution |

## Reading

The two dominant mechanisms are evidence misinterpretation and evidence disconnected from action. Together they cover 0.561039 of failed rows when counted as a union. Evidence absence exists, but it is not the main failure source in the cloud-only aligned set.
